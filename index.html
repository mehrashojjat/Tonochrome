Blind Mode

This mode is designed for blind and low-vision users. Here is what happens when you continue:

  • Your device will ask for camera permission. Please allow it so the app can read the color from what your camera sees.
  • The app continuously detects the color your camera is pointing at and converts it to sound.
  • Tap the Play button at the bottom of the screen to start hearing the color as a sound. Tap Stop to silence it at any time.
  • The sound changes in real time as you move the camera toward different colors.
  • A Flash button will appear at the top of the camera view if your device supports it, so you can illuminate what you are pointing at.

After tapping OK, a second screen will explain exactly what each sound means — which pitches represent which colors, and what the noise layers tell you about brightness and vividness.

Sound and Color Guide

How pitch tells you the color

The pitch of the sound — how high or low it is — tells you the hue, the color family. Pitch rises smoothly in this order from lowest to highest:

  1. Lowest pitch — Deep Red.
  2. Low pitch — Orange.
  3. Low-medium pitch — Yellow. Bright, warm tones.
  4. Medium pitch — Green. This is the middle of the pitch range.
  5. Medium-high pitch — Cyan and Teal.
  6. High pitch — Blue. Cool tones at the higher notes.
  7. Very high pitch — Violet and Purple.
  8. At the very highest pitches the sound blends back toward Red again, completing the circle of hues.

How noise tells you about color vividness

The vividness of a color — called saturation — changes how clean or noisy the tone sounds.

  • A clean, pure tone with no noise means the color is fully vivid and saturated — a strong, rich shade like a bright red, a deep blue, or a vivid green.
  • A tone with some roughness or noise mixed in means the color is less vivid — a muted, faded, or dusty shade.
  • A mostly or completely noisy sound with very little clear pitch means the color is nearly grey or completely grey, with almost no color at all.

How noise tells you about brightness

The brightness of a color — called lightness — is carried by a separate noise layer on top of or under the main tone.

  • A warm, low rumbling noise underneath the tone means the color is very dark — a near-black or deep dark shade.
  • A bright, airy hissing noise layered over the tone means the color is very bright — a near-white or heavily washed-out pale shade.
  • No noise at all, just a clear tone at a comfortable volume, means the color is a true mid-brightness, naturally visible shade.

Quick reference summary

  • Low pitch, clean tone — dark or vivid Red.
  • Medium pitch, clean tone — vivid Green.
  • High pitch, clean tone — vivid Blue or Purple.
  • Any pitch, very noisy — grey or nearly grey with low saturation.
  • Any pitch, with a warm low rumble — dark or near-black shade.
  • Any pitch, with a bright hiss — light or near-white shade.

Photo Mode

In Photo Mode you explore colors in a photo by listening to their sounds.

  • Tap Pick Image at the bottom to choose a photo from your device gallery.
  • Once the photo loads, tap Play to enable audio.
  • Then tap or drag your finger anywhere on the image to hear the sound of the color at that spot. The sound updates in real time as you move.
  • The current color being played is shown in the color bar above the image.
  • Tap Stop at any time to silence the audio.
  • Accessibility note: If audio does not respond when you tap, your finger may be outside the image boundary. Move toward the center of the screen.

Advance Mode

Advance Mode gives you full manual control over color sounds using three sliders.

  • The Hue slider controls pitch. Moving it left to right shifts the sound from deep red through orange, yellow, green, blue, and into violet.
  • The Saturation slider controls the vividness or greyness of the tone. Low saturation adds a bell-like resonance. High saturation gives a clean tone.
  • The Lightness slider controls volume and layered noise texture. Dark values add a low rumble. Mid-range plays a clear tone. Bright values layer an airy hiss.
  • The Advanced Settings panel lets you choose instrument presets, sound modes, and fine-tune all audio parameters.

Note for blind users: This mode requires manually moving three sliders and has no automatic color detection. If you have low or no vision, Blind Mode is strongly recommended instead — it works without any slider interaction.
